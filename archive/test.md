This persistence enables high-skill forecasting via simple tools. The dataset revealed episodic breaks within the persistent pattern (e.g., early July blowing dust), underscoring the value of recent METAR trends and antecedent conditions over raw ensemble outputs.

These learnings directly enhanced my subsystem accuracy: prioritizing climatology-validated manual calculations (nomogram + METAR-derived inputs) over potentially biased models (e.g., noted ECMWF superiority in theater), while integrating topographic-thermal forcing hierarchies. Such iterations exemplify how expert input drives innovation in Earth Science AI applications—improving outcomes in specialized forecasting, hazard assessment, and broader geophysical understanding.
